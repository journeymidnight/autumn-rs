Symptom (user-reported, 2026-04-27 23:00):
  After driving the 4-disk EC cluster through wbench + split tests,
  `cluster.sh stop && start` showed `top -pid <ps>` reporting 16 GB
  memory footprint (RSS 2 MB, compressor 8.6 GB, 17.6 M swapouts).
  Investigating /tmp/autumn-rs-logs/ps.log:
    - part 15: logStream end=2.46 GB, vp_offset=499 MB → 1.96 GB to
      replay on open
    - part 36: logStream end=1.09 GB, vp_offset=639 MB → 448 MB
    - parts 19/28/44: vp_offset == end (already drained)
  GC for extent 22 (sealed=2.77 GB) ran 4 m 16 s; VP resolves spiked
  to 4.4 s due to swap pressure. The replay window itself is the root
  cause — `imm: VecDeque<Arc<Memtable>>` was unbounded.

Diagnosis:
  Three independent gaps in the partition server:
    1. `rotate_active` (lib.rs:2625) does an unconditional
       `imm.push_back` against an unbounded VecDeque. P-bulk falling
       behind on `row_stream.append` (especially with EC encoding)
       lets imm grow without limit. RocksDB caps this at
       `max_write_buffer_number=2` and stalls writes when full.
    2. `maybe_rotate` only fires on `active.mem_bytes >=
       FLUSH_MEM_BYTES = 256 MB`. Workloads of small writes that
       never fill the memtable can still grow log_stream without
       bound — VPs cost ~16 B in memtable but the full payload sits
       in log_stream. RocksDB's `max_total_wal_size` (default
       `write_buffer_size × max_write_buffer_number × 4 ≈ 2 GB`)
       handles this exact case.
    3. `cluster.sh stop` sends SIGTERM, waits 5 s, then SIGKILL.
       `autumn-ps` had no signal handler. `merged_partition_loop`'s
       tail-drain only flushed `pending` + `inflight` — it did NOT
       rotate `active` or drain `imm`, so any data ack'd to the
       client between the last flush and SIGKILL was left for replay.

    intact.

    crosses thread boundaries.

    compio thread, sender side; receiver side polled by
    merged_partition_loop on the partition thread. The
    `oneshot::Sender<()>` payload is Send by construction.

    + its `flush_one_imm_local` fallback, so degraded-mode partitions
    still drain correctly.
  - `imm: VecDeque<Arc<Memtable>>` stays Arc — FlushReq sends the
    Memtable cross-thread to P-bulk; user explicitly considered Rc
